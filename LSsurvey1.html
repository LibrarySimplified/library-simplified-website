The Library Simplified Summary was design was infomred by existing national surveys to understand library patron use of ebooks. For the first round, 50% of the partner libraries participated. There were almost 2000 responces to the survey with the over 50% of reponces comming from Kent District Library. The remaining balance of respondants came from Sacramento Public Libary, Santa Clara Public Library, Alemeda Public Library and Brooklyn Public Library. A subsequent survey building upon this initial survey is under construction to improve upon and validate this survey's findings accros the remaining libraries. Additionally, this subsequent survey may expose any point in time bias that may have been introduced in the first round as well as further explore findings in the first survey.

The first section is a general overview of eBook readers, including some comparison against non-eBook readers. The second is attempting to get at a "user profile" by doing a side-by-side comparison on demographic cuts of income, gender, and library usage behavior.

A hard copy of the report can be downloaded here.

eReaders - Summary

  1. eReaders most often use the library to look for print books
  2. On average, eReaders visit the library slightly less than non-eReaders
  3. Generally, eReaders are reading for the same purposes as non-eReaders, with pleasure reading most dominant
  4. eReaders prefer eBooks for their speed and convenienceâ€”they are most often the preferred format for travel and bed, while print is still preferred greatly for kids and sharing
  5. The majority of eReaders look for recommendations from
    • friends (92%)
    • online bookstores (84%)
    • librarians (61%)
      • seeking more recommendations across sources than non-eReaders
  6. About half of eReaders read newspapers and magazines in digital formats
  7. Just over half (52%) of eReaders use multiple devices to read
  8. Most eReaders are using a tablet (58%) or an eReader (50%), while some are using their smartphone or a PC
  9. 45% of eReaders own a Kindle, and 20% own a Nook
  10. On average, eReaders are wealthier, more likely to be employed, and have higher levels of educations than non-eReaders
